Sri Lanka has imposed a curfew following eight explosions on Easter Sunday at churches and hotels that killed more than 200 people and wounded hundreds more.

The targets of the bombings were Catholic worshippers attending Easter prayers at churches in three cities and tourists.

The archbishop of Colombo who leads two of the churches targeted condemned the attacks and called on Sri Lankan people not to take the law into their own hands.

"I also like to ask the government to hold a very impartial and strong inquiry and find out who is responsible behind this act, and also to punish them, mercilessly, because only animals can behave like that."

Around 30 foreigners including American and British citizens were among those dead and injured in the attack.

Washington remains focused on steps Congress may take after a redacted report from special counsel Robert Mueller's Russia probe was released last week.

VOA's Michael Bowman reports, Democrats in the House of Representatives are determined to obtain a complete, unredacted version of the document, which found no collusion between President Trump's inner circle and Russia, but [neither established] neither established nor ruled out that the president obstructed justice.

Gunmen attacked a Malian army base in a dawn raid on Sunday, killing 11 soldiers and burning the camp in west-central Mali. That's according to the army and a local lawmaker.

No group has yet claimed responsibility for the attack. Central Mali has in the past few years been overrun by jihadists with links to al-Qaeda.



Sudanese protest leaders on Sunday vowed to escalate demonstrations to confront the country's military rulers. This is part of a widening campaign to push for a transfer of power to civilians.

A protest leader said at a rally in Khartoum that demonstrators no longer recognized the Transitional Military Council that assumed power after the ousting of President Omar al-Bashir last week.

The Sudanese Professionals Association spokesperson Mohamed al-Amin said this new phase will include continuing sit-ins and end of negotiations and for revolutionary escalation in the streets. He said that will go on until all demands are met.

Hundreds of thousands of protesters attended the rally, waving flags while nationalistic music blared from stage-mounted speakers.
