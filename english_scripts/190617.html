Amid rising tensions in the Persian Gulf, Secretary Pompeo told Fox News on Sunday that Washington would guarantee freedom of navigation for shipping through the Strait of Hormuz.

"This is an international challenge. This is important to the entire globe. The United States is gonna make sure that we take all the actions necessary, diplomatic and otherwise, that achieve that outcome."

Pompeo contended that it was unmistakable that Iran launched last week's attacks on two tankers sailing through the narrow passage.

"Our allies warned the United States, I think our intelligence agencies warned policymakers, that this kind of Iranian reaction was likely a result of a policy of withdrawing from the Iran Nuclear Agreement. The mass civil disobedience campaign in Khartoum is impeding aid from flowing to flood-stricken victims in the northern Darfur region, according to the United States Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs.

Lisa Schlein [reports] reports for VOA from OCHA headquarters in Geneva.

The U.N. reports heavy flooding in Tawilla in North Darfur has completely destroyed the homes of some 2,500 people and affected nearly 900 latrines, which poses an immediate health hazard.

OCHA spokesperson Jens Laerke says flood victims are in urgent need of shelter, food and non-food items and health services. But he notes humanitarian operations are being hampered by unstable conditions in Khartoum.

"That means that some of the cash that needs to go to Darfur is not arriving. So, they simply can't pay locally for the services."

In addition to this natural disaster, Laerke tells VOA violent intercommunal clashes broke out in Deleij in central Darfur a few days ago.

Lisa Schlein also reports that U.N. humanitarian operation in Sudan are struggling for lack of money. Less than one quarter of the ($)1.2 billion United Nations appeal for Sudan is funded.



And the United States, the battle to contain the worst measles outbreak in 27 years has a new front: summer camp.

AP correspondent Julie Walker reports.

Of the 170 camps in Sullivan County, north of New York City, 139 of them are Orthodox Jewish, and with that community hit hardest by the measles outbreak, Sullivan County spokesman Dan Hust says they've taken the extra step to make vaccinations mandatory for all campers and staff.

"Our greatest fear, of course, is that it would magnify into a much larger outbreak."

The public health order from Sullivan County as well as a similar order in Orange County have been challenged in court. But legal experts believe those cases will have to be withdrawn, given New York's removal of religious exemptions for vaccinations.

The wife of Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has been ordered to pay a $15,000 fine after she admitted to misuse of state funds.
