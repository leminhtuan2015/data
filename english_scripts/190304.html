Commanders of the U.S.-backed forces in Syria say they are engaged in intense fighting Sunday as they battle for the last shred of the Islamic State's self-declared caliphate in Syria.

Earlier Sunday, a Syrian Democratic Force military media official told the Reuters news agency they expect the fighting near the Iraqi border will be a decisive battle.

Both U.S. and SDF officials have been bracing for a difficult fight, warning that those IS fighters who remained in Baghuz, the town where they were fighting, were among the most dedicated and hardened that the terror group had to offer.

Top Democratic lawmakers promised on Sunday that they would step up their investigations of President Trump and his connections to Russia during the 2016 presidential election.

House Judiciary Committee Chairman Jerrold Nadler told ABC News that on Monday his committee would be issuing requests for documents to 60 people "to begin the investigations to present the case to the American people about obstruction of justice, corruption and abuse of power."

Nadler said the requests would be sent to officials at the White House and the Justice Department, along with Trump's oldest son, Donald Trump Jr., and Allen Weisselberg, who is the chief financial officer of the Trump Organization, the president's global business empire.
