Forces loyal to rival Libyan army commander Khalifa Haftar said Saturday they had seized control of the main airport in Libya's capital, Tripoli.

As Edward Yeranian reports, that came two days after Haftar ordered his forces to seize the seat of Libya's U.N.-backed government.

Arab media broadcast video of General Haftar's forces entering the gates of Tripoli's now-closed international airport before taking positions outside terminal buildings and along the now-unused runways. There did not appear to be any resistance to the takeover.

U.N. special envoy Ghassan Salame, who is now in Libya, said that he was monitoring the situation closely.

He says that tensions were increasing in a number of places in and around Tripoli and that he was keeping tabs on the situation minute by minute. He insists that it is urgent to put an end to tensions in those areas and to make sure civilians are not harmed.

Edward Yeranian, for VOA news, Cairo.



Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu said Saturday that he would annex Israeli settlements in the occupied West Bank if he wins another term in office, a last-minute pre-election promise likely to enrage Palestinians and the Arab world.

Netanyahu is competing for votes with small far-right parties who advocate annexation. His comments are likely to appeal to hardline voters, who object to ceding lands to the Palestinians.

A spokesman for Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas, Nabil Abu Rdainah, said that "Any measures and any announcements will not change the facts. Settlements are illegal and they will be removed."

At least seven policemen and three civilians were killed Saturday in a series of attacks in Afghanistan.

In the eastern Ghazni province, Taliban insurgents killed three policemen in attacks on security checkpoints.

At least four policemen were killed when the Taliban stormed a security checkpoint in the northern Sari Pul province, according to a provincial council leader.

Two explosions in the eastern Nangarhar province claimed the lives of at least three civilians, according to a spokesman for the provincial governor.

No groups claimed responsibility for the attacks in Nangarhar and Sari Pul.



President Donald Trump doubled down on his latest assertion that the U.S. immigration system is "full" and cannot take any more migrants from Central America.

Speaking to the Republican Jewish Coalition in Las Vegas Saturday, Mr. Trump said the immigration system is broken with criminals and drug dealers posing as those seeking asylum.

"You look at this guy, you say, wow, that's a tough cookie. I am very fearful for my life. I am very worried that I will be accosted if I'm sent back home. No, no. He'll do the accosting."

Trump was quoting Jewish voters for next year's election. American Jews have traditionally voted with the Democratic Party, but the president was trying to highlight some of his actions including his decision to move the U.S. embassy in Israel from Tel Aviv to Jerusalem and recognize Israeli sovereignty over the Golan Heights.



The Trump administration wants up to two years to find all the children separated from their parents at the U.S.-Mexico border.

AP's Julie Walker has details.

In court filings, the Justice Department says it needs to review 47,000 cases of unaccompanied children taken into custody at the border between June, 2017 and July, 2018. The administration would begin by sifting through names for signs of likely separation - for example, children under 5 years old.

The administration would then provide this information to the American Civil Liberties Union, which sued to reunite families. The ACLU says there is no reason it should take up to two years.

I'm Julie Walker.



And rival political factions took to the streets across Venezuela on Saturday in a mounting struggle for control of the crisis-wracked nation, where U.S.-backed opposition leader Juan Guaidó is attempting to oust socialist President Nicholás Maduro.

Thousands crammed into the El Marques district of Caracas in support of Guaidó, the leader of the National Assembly. Meanwhile, across town, thousands of Maduro backers, all dressed in red, waved flags and danced around the stage, blasting music ahead of a march to the presidential palace.
