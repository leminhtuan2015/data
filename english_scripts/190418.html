The United Nations said Wednesday that the Libyan capital saw its most intense fighting overnight since the forces of General Khalifa Haftar began their battle to take Tripoli earlier this month.

A spokesman told reporters in New York that at least five civilians were reportedly killed and several others injured.

The U.N.'s top diplomat in Libya condemned the shelling saying, "Horrible night of random shelling of residential areas. For the sake of 3 million civilians living in Greater Tripoli, these attacks should stop. NOW!"



The United States announced new sanctions on Venezuela, Cuba and Nicaragua on Wednesday.

AP correspondent Sagar Meghani reports.

Secretary of State Mike Pompeo says the administration's implementing a 1996 act to give Americans the right to sue mostly European companies operating at a property Cuba seized when Fidel Castro took power.

Every president since Bill Clinton had suspended the right in part to avoid trade clashes with Europeans who are now vowing to protect their businesses.

The U.S. says there will be no exceptions.

"Any person or company doing business in Cuba should heed this announcement."

The move could effect dozens of Canadian and European companies to the tune of tens of billions of dollars.

Sagar Meghani, at the White House.

A six-decade old economic embargo of Cuba by the United States remains in effect.



In Venezuela, people are welcoming the arrival of humanitarian aid from the Red Cross and the Red Crescent Society.

The United Nations says a quarter of Venezuela's 30 million people urgently need food and medical help.



Officials in Peru say former President Alan Garcia has died after he shot himself as police arrived to arrest him over bribery allegations.

He had repeatedly denied wrongdoing.

Garcia had served two five-year terms as the country's president.

Reports citing relatives of former Sudanese President Omar al-Bashir say the ousted leader has been moved from house arrest to prison. But protesters who are demanding civilian leadership, instead of the transitional military council, are skeptical.

Correspondent Naba Mohiedeen reports from Khartoum.

Reports on Wednesday said the ousted former president of Sudan, Omar al-Bashir, has been moved from house arrest to federal prison.

Unnamed members of Bashir's family told Reuters news agency, the BBC, and others he was moved to Kobar prison in Khartoum late Tuesday, while prison guards told CNN they saw Bashir's arrival at the facility.

Kobar prison is where Bashir locked up many of his opponents during his three decades in power.



A young woman from the southeastern American state of Florida who traveled to the western state of Colorado and bought a shotgun for what authorities feared would be a attack on a high school has died in an apparent suicide after a nearly 24-hour manhunt.

Jefferson County Sheriff Jeff Shrader said the 18-year-old woman, Sol Pais, was discovered by the FBI with an apparent self-inflicted gunshot wound.

The manhunt had led to the closing of schools in the area as a precaution.

During the manhunt, the FBI had said Pais was "infatuated" with the Columbine High School and made threats ahead of the anniversary of the attack that killed 13 people - students and teachers - at Columbine High School in 1999. The FBI had described her "extremely dangerous."
