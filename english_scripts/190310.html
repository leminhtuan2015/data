Nigerians went to the polls Saturday to choose governors in 29 of 36 states in Africa's largest democracy. Turnout appeared to be low.

Just like the country's presidential elections, Saturday's vote had been postponed by a week in a last minute decision as the electoral commission cited numerous logistical problems in the country of 190 million people with poor infrastructure.

French riot police used water cannon and fired tear gas against yellow vest protesters in Paris Saturday afternoon, the 17th straight weekend of anti-government demonstrations.

As during previous demos by the movement, the protest began at the Arc de Triomphe, looped through both sides of the Seine River and then ended at the top of the Luxembourg Gardens on the Left Bank.

Marches were also held in numerous cities around France, including Bordeaux, which has a very strong contingent of yellow vest protesters, and Lille.

The protests began in November against an increase in gas taxes but have become a movement against French President Emmanuel Macron's economic policies, which the protesters say favor the wealthy over ordinary French citizens.



The head of the World Health Organization has denounced a deadly attack Saturday on an Ebola treatment center in the eastern Democratic Republic of Congo.

Gunmen attacked the center at Butembo, North Kivu province, early Saturday, killing a policeman and wounding a health worker.

WHO Director General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus, speaking after a four-hour visit to the clinic, told reporters that the violence in the region was undermining early progress that had been made in fighting an Ebola outbreak.

"As you know, we are at a pivotal moment in this outbreak, and all the partners will not relent until we put an end to this outbreak."

The Butembo facility only opened last Saturday after an attack by gunmen the previous Wednesday had forced it to close.
