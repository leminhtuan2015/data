Armed rebels attacked an Ebola treatment center Saturday near the Congolese city of Butembo just hours after another attack at that same center killed a Cameroon epidemiologist.

One militia member was killed in the attack at Katwa hospital where medical staffers are trying to contain an Ebola outbreak in the eastern part of the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Four rebels were captured.

The latest attack came after the World Health Organization reported Friday that epidemiologist Richard Mouzoko was killed when several gunmen from a local militia stormed the hospital.



Libya has closed Tripoli's only functioning airport after residents reported an airstrike on the capital.

The airport's website said the Civil Aviation Authority closed the airspace of the Matiga airport until further notice for the sake of air security. It added that the airport had not been hit.



Egyptians began three days of voting Saturday on constitutional amendments that would allow Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el-Sissi to stay in office until 2030.

Edward Yeranian reports.

A smiling President Abdel Fattah el-Sissi cast his ballot as polling began Saturday morning in a three-day referendum (to) make changes to Egypt's post-revolution constitution.

Those changes will strengthen his hand over parts of the government and lengthen his term in office from four to six years.

Sixty-two million Egyptians are registered to vote of an estimated population of around 100 million.

A large number of people could be seen at several polling stations in the center of Cairo while others in outlying parts of the capital and Giza appeared to have a light turnout.

Edward Yeranian, for VOA news, Cairo.

Yellow vest protesters set fires on a march through Paris Saturday as they sought to drive home their message to the Macron government that they believe is ignoring the poor.

We get more from AP's Ben Thomas.

Tear gas hung in the air throughout the afternoon Saturday around the Place de la République in Paris.

Radical protesters hurled paving stones and flares, attacked at least one boarded-up store and set fire to a few vehicles.

Police repeatedly charged in an effort to contain the crowd.

Associated Press reporters saw at least two journalists injured in the melee.

Meanwhile, a separate smaller crowd of yellow vests marched peacefully toward the cordoned-off neighborhood around Notre Dame. They are urging the French government not to forget the poor even as it gathers millions to rebuild the fire-damaged cathedral.

I'm Ben Thomas.

Mourners marked 20 years Saturday since the mass shooting at Columbine High School in Colorado.

AP correspondent Mike Gracia recounts the advance of that day.

It was a shocking event. The date was April 20, 1999. Two disaffected young men, both teenagers and students at the school, opened fire at Columbine High School in Littleton, Colorado. They killed 12 fellow students and a teacher and wounded two dozen others before taking their own lives.

In the aftermath, there were questions of why and how could this happen.

One month later, President Bill Clinton told Columbine students "What happened to you has pierced the soul of America."

I'm Mike Gracia.



A zookeeper in Topeka, Kansas, was mauled by a Sumatran tiger named Sanjiv at the zoo Saturday.

Zoo officials say the unidentified worker was working in the tiger's enclosure when she was attacked.

The zoo director says there are no repercussions planned for the animal.

The woman was taken to a local hospital where she is in stable condition with wounds to her neck, back and arms.
