At least five Afghan troops were killed by friendly fire Tuesday night in what was supposed to be a precision airstrike by U.S. forces supporting troops battling insurgents in the country's south.

In a statement released on Wednesday, the Afghan Defense Ministry said the mishap happened near the city of Tarin Kot in Uruzgan province and said it resulted from miscommunication between the Afghan troops on the ground and forces in the air.



Dozens of children were believed trapped inside a building in Lagos, Nigeria, where hundreds of onlookers gathered to watch a massive rescue attempt.

It's unclear how many people are inside the ruins of the three-story building that collapsed at about 10:00 a.m. local time Wednesday.

Onlookers said a school with about 100 students was located on the building's top floor. Several children were rescued from the debris and the crowd watching cheered when that news reached them.

Ambulances, fire trucks and heavy building equipment were standing by to help with the search-and-rescue effort.

There is no word on how many adults were in the building when it fell.

The building was located on the Ita-faji are of Lagos Island, which is the oldest part of the city.



The governor of São Paulo, Brazil, says two young men wearing hoods and carrying several weapons opened fired at a school in the southern part of the country, killing eight people before they then took their own lives.

Wednesday's shooting happened in a public school in Suzano, a suburb of São Paulo, Brazil's largest city.

The governor says the two attackers were believed to be between 20 and 25 years old. He says authorities don't believe they were former students.

President Trump's former campaign chairman Paul Manafort was sentenced to a longer term in prison on Wednesday for conspiracy linked to his lobbying efforts in Ukraine for a pro-Russia political party.

AP [correspondent] Washington correspondent Sagar Meghani reports from the White House.

Manafort was sentenced last week to roughly four years in prison in a separate case and will serve about three and a half years on top of that.

Manafort had pleaded guilty to two counts of conspiracy which could have handed him 10 years in prison.

Judge Amy Berman Jackson handed down the sentence after saying she'd give Manafort credit for accepting responsibility. But she also laced into him over the crimes.

Manafort earlier read a statement pleading for leniency, saying he's sorry for what he's done and asked the judge to not give him more jail time and instead let him get back to being his wife's primary caregiver.

Sagar Meghani, at the White House.



Islamic State fighters launched new counterattacks against U.S.-backed forces Wednesday as the militants clung to the terror group's last scrap of territory in northeastern Syria.

Officials with both the United States and the Syrian Democratic Forces say they've been taking cover from airstrikes and artillery fire in a complex system of caves and tunnels below ground.



President Trump attacked the governor of California on Wednesday for his plan to impose a state moratorium on carrying out death sentences against convicted killers.

On Twitter, Trump said, "Defying voters, the Governor of California will halt all death penalty executions of 737 stone cold killers."
