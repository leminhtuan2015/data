U.S. President Donald Trump criticized opposition Democrats Tuesday for opening a wide range investigation of him and his administration.

"It's a disgrace, it's a disgrace for our country. I'm not surprised that it's happening. Basically, they've started the campaign."

The president also tweeted it was "presidential harassment."

On Monday, House of Representatives Democrats demanded documents from 81 people and entities linked to Trump's 2016 campaign, his businesses and more. The House panel said it is investigating obstruction of justice, possible public corruption and abuses of power during Trump's two-year presidency.

The president has long denied wrongdoing involving his campaign's links to Russia and during his presidency.



Trump says he will visit with survivors of violent tornadoes that killed 23 people in the southern state of Alabama on Friday.

Trump made the announcement at the White House Tuesday as rescue workers continued to sift through the rubble left by the Sunday storms in search of victims.

"It's been a tragic situation, but a lot of good work is being done. I'm in constant touch with the governor and also the governor of Georgia. And so we'll be going to the area."

The victims range in age from 6 to 93.



London's counterterrorism police said they had launched an investigation into who mailed three small bombs to two airports and a major rail station on Tuesday.

No one was injured by the devices, one of which caused a small fire in an office building at Heathrow Airport.

Later, a similar package was identified in the post room of London's busiest rail station Waterloo.

And a third was found in an office at London City Airport in East London.

Venezuelan President Nicholás Maduro said on Tuesday he would defeat a "crazed minority" determined to destabilize the country.

These were Maduro's first public comments since opposition leader Juan Guaidó defied him by returning on Monday.

During a ceremony to commemorate the sixth anniversary of the death of his predecessor Hugo Chávez, Maduro called on supporters to attend "anti-imperialist" demonstrations on March 9, coinciding with an opposition march announced by Guaidó.



A Japanese court Tuesday approved the release of former Nissan Chairman Carlos Ghosn from prison. But his release was delayed when prosecutors filed a last-minute appeal to the court's decision.

The AP's Charles De Ledesma reports.

Bail for Ghosn was set at $8.9 million. But the end of his four months of detention in Japan has had to be put back after prosecutors appealed the decision.

A lawyer for Ghosn said he would not be able to leave the Tokyo Detention Center until Wednesday at the earliest even if the appeal is rejected because bail procedures can't be done at night.

The acceptance of Ghosn's request for bail, his third, comes after his lawyer said he was confident the auto executive's bid would succeed.

The former head of the Nissan, Mitsubishi Motors, Renault alliance has been in prison since his November arrest on charges of falsifying financial statements for nearly a decade.



Hundreds of Islamic State fighters and family members fled the northeastern Syrian village of Baghuz Tuesday. They were taking advantage of a pause in fighting with the U.S.-backed forces that have surrounded the last patch of the terror group's self-declared caliphate.

Officials with the Syrian Democratic Forces, Kurdish militias and human rights monitors confirmed the exodus late Monday though the number of fighters and civilians vary.



South Sudan's president is calling on his people to refrain from revenge attacks as the country tries to move on from a brutal civil war.

Speaking Tuesday in the town of Wau, Salva Kiir said that with a peace deal in place, people must try to reconcile and peacefully co-exist.

Kiir also said the conflict was caused by rivalries in the ruling SPLM party though he declined to take personal responsibility.
