Hong Kong's chief executive Carrie Lam offered an apology Tuesday for the political crisis and unrest sparked by a controversial extradition bill. The bill would have allowed extradition to mainland China.

Lam says work on the bill was over until it expires in July next year, and she pledged to stay and finish the remainder of her term.

"I personally have to shoulder much of the responsibility."

An apology was not enough for some of those still protesting. Members of the Civil Human Rights Front in Hong Kong say they are angry that Lam is refusing to step down.

"After millions of Hong Kong people continue to join the rally protest, nearly hundred of them have been sent to hospital. Some of them have been arrested. And we are not sure when we will face sort of prosecution."

Opposition to the bill has driven repeated mass protests in Hong Kong this month. Organizers said two million people turned out on Sunday.
